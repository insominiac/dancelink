African Roots

The journey from Angola to the world

Born in Luanda

Kizomba emerged in Angola during the late 1970s and early 1980s, born from the fusion of traditional Angolan Semba with Caribbean Zouk music. The word "Kizomba" means "party" in Kimbundu, one of Angola's national languages.

Cultural Heritage

More than just a dance, Kizomba represents the resilience and joy of Angolan culture. It emerged during a time of civil war, serving as a form of expression and unity for the Angolan people. The dance embodies the African philosophy of connection - between music, movement, and soul.

Evolution & Styles

How Kizomba has transformed and diversified

Traditional Kizomba

The Original Form

Characterized by smooth, grounded movements with a close embrace. The dance maintains a strong connection to its Semba roots, emphasizing the walking patterns and subtle hip movements that define authentic Angolan style.

Urban Kiz

The French Evolution

Developed in Paris in the late 2000s, Urban Kiz introduced a more upright posture, linear movements, and influences from hip-hop and R&B. This style emphasizes musical interpretation with breaks, isolations, and syncopated footwork.

Tarraxo/Tarraxinha

The Sensual Variant

An extremely slow and sensual style that focuses on body isolations and minimal footwork. Partners maintain an intimate connection while interpreting the music through subtle body movements and waves.
